Background

Waking up in the morning and getting out the door shouldn’t require opening three different applications. We want our users (commuters) to have all the information they need for starting their day at their fingertips right when they wake up.

Normally, people would turn off their alarm in their Alarms app, then check the Weather app for the weather that day. Next, they'll open up Google Maps to check the route or traffic. Users shouldn’t have to open three separate apps just to get data relevant to their day-to-day life.

Storyboarding

We created storyboards to get a better idea of the people who might want to use our application. We used these to construct personas with goals and needs that might be met with Alarmio.

Prototyping

Next, we created two different paper prototypes. One shows a design for our home screen with several cards listed. Each card represents one feature available - alarms, weather, traffic, motivation, etc. We decided to focus on the first three as the main features of our app. A demonstration of the second paper prototype can be seen in the video linked below.

Wireframes

We took the best features from each of our two paper prototypes to create a low-fidelity wireframe. We were able to make it interactive using Marvel and see the user workflow of the app.

User Testing

After we coded a working prototype of our app, we conducted user testing. Our user testing plan can be found here. Users helped us find usability bugs and gave us a lot of helpful feedback at this stage, such as making certain areas clickable and fixing the back button. After we received feedback from our users, we noted the points we needed to fix and compiled a list of changes to utilize for our app.

Summary of Findings

After conducting user testing, we found that users were generally able to navigate easily through our app to add new alarms and new locations to check the weather. However, each of them had issues with the back button after adding an alarm. They expect to be brought back to the home page, but instead are brought back to the previous page they were on (an empty page for adding an alarm).

List of Changes

  • Fix back button so that it goes back to the home page after adding a new alarm or location, instead of going back to the page that lets you add a new alarm or location
  • Make the arrow on the home page clickable as well as the heading name (i.e. the entire ALARMS > would lead to the Alarms page rather than just the heading name)
  • Change the time input form to a scrolling time for users to easily input the time instead of typing it in
  • Have AM / PM as buttons next to the scrolling time input, with AM as the default selected button
  • Move all the “Add” buttons (Add Alarm, Add Location, Add Trip) from the left to the right
  • Hide Latitude and Longitude, as it is never used

Final Design

We incorporated most of the changes and feedback we received from user testing to the best of our ability into our final design.
